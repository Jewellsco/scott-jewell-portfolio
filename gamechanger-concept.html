GameChanger, a concept for the Stats and Insights team

Worth every
dollar

GameChanger families pay about 99 dollars a year, and their reviews say they feel it. The same families generate nine million games of scoring data a season. This concept returns that data as felt value, so the subscription justifies itself every week.

How this was made. Built from a design teardown of GameChanger's public properties, both app stores, help documentation, and press. Direction was set in low fidelity first, the process files sit behind this page. All teams, players, and numbers are fictional.

The evidence

Four findings, all theirs

Their homepage

The capture machine

Nine million plus games a year across a million plus teams, scored pitch by pitch by volunteer scorekeepers. The richest dataset in youth sports.

Google Play reviews

The price is felt

"Extremely pricey, very greedy." Prices rose, family plans roughly doubled, and paying subscribers report seeing ads. The bill arrives before the value does.

App Store reviews

Demand is not the problem

Reviewers push for deeper stats and scoring features faster than they arrive. Families are asking their data to do more.

Help center and press

Insights already shipping

Post game insights, lineup recommendations in beta, Film Room video analysis. The company is already betting on this surface.

Resented price, rich data, proven demand. That is one job description. Make the data feel worth the money.

The idea

Every sentence carries its receipt

One engine turns captured numbers into plain sentences for whoever is reading, and every sentence keeps its raw number one tap behind it. Simple on the surface, honest underneath, valuable weekly.

Two insights, never more. The moment a recap scrolls, it becomes a stat page and the reader is gone. Ruthless selection is the feature.
The receipt is one tap behind. The sentence earns trust because the number is always right there, a comparison first, the full history under it. Depth is earned by taps, not dumped.
The share is the growth loop. A sentence a grandparent understands is a sentence a parent forwards. Every share is a free advertisement written by the data.
Measure, recap open rate

Chunk two

Decisions with receipts

The coach surface. One suggestion before practice, reasons always visible, because a volunteer coach will only use what they can defend to a parent. This maps to their Lineup Recommendations beta.

Reasons before buttons. The reasoning is the interface. A suggestion that cannot show its work does not ship, that is the trust bar for every recommendation this team makes.
Declining is a first class action. Not this week is data too, it teaches the engine and it tells the coach the tool respects their judgment.

The close

From resented price to obvious value

The scorekeeping engine already works, the reviews prove it. What families are missing is the return on their own data. Two insights a week, receipts always one tap behind, and a coach who can defend every suggestion. That is what worth every dollar looks like.
