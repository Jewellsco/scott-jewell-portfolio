Overview

A horizon project, one page at a time

Freebird's site redesign was a long-term, multi-team effort. Rather than boil the ocean, we narrowed focus and tackled the product page first — the page that does the most commercial work. As lead, I owned the product vision and thinking, partnering with a PM, three backend developers, a UX researcher, and stakeholders from Marketing, eCommerce, and Social.

"Big redesigns die in scope. We picked the one page that mattered most and made it undeniable."
The redesign at a glance
Process

Buy-in, audit, then design

1

Competitive analysis

Studied how the best retail product pages convert, building the evidence base to align stakeholders before any pixels moved.

2

Secure stakeholder buy-in

A redesign this visible needed agreement across Marketing, eCommerce, and Social. I made the case so research could turn into design.

3

Audit the current page

Documented exactly what was outdated and the specific updates the page needed — a clear, prioritized punch list.

4

Redesign the critical screens

Reworked the product page (and related critical pages) against the new direction, aligned to the design system.

Reflection

What I took from it

The biggest lever wasn't visual — it was focus. Picking one high-impact page and building consensus around it turned a stalled "redesign everything" ambition into shipped progress. Leading the vision also meant doing the unglamorous work: the competitive analysis and the stakeholder conversations that make design possible.
