Overview

A blank canvas in a high trust category

BagEquity is a fintech platform connecting families and youth sports coaches around shared money. As the founding designer, I'm responsible for the entire product experience, onboarding, accounts, money movement, plus the design system that keeps it all consistent as the product grows. Money plus minors plus multiple parties is one of the highest trust problems in product design.

"How do you make moving money between families and coaches feel as simple as a text, while meeting the bar a regulated fintech requires?"
The Challenge

Simple on the surface, serious underneath

There were no existing patterns to inherit. Every screen had to earn trust on first contact, handle sensitive family and minor data responsibly, and satisfy identity and consent requirements, all without burying users in steps or jargon. And it had to be built on a system that one designer could scale, not a pile of one off screens.

My Process

Designing zero to one

1

Framing the problem

Defined who the product serves, families and coaches, and the core jobs to be done, mapping the full money movement journey before drawing a single screen.

2

Flows & architecture

Designed onboarding, account, and transaction flows with security and consent treated as part of the experience, not bolted on afterward.

3

Design system from scratch

Built the UI kit and component library in Figma, with tokens, patterns, and accessible (WCAG) components, so the product stays coherent and fast to extend.

4

Prototype, test, hand off

Validated flows through interactive prototypes and usability checks, then partnered with engineering on dev ready handoff.

The Solution

A coherent, trustworthy product system

The result is a unified experience across web and mobile, with clear onboarding, confident money movement flows, and compliance aware identity and consent that feel reassuring rather than bureaucratic, all powered by a scalable design system that lets the product grow without losing its voice.

Reflection

What I took from it

Founding the design meant wearing every hat, strategy, research, systems, and pixels. The biggest lesson, in a high trust category, design is the trust. The flows that look simplest are the ones that did the most work to earn confidence. Building the system early is what let one designer ship a product that still feels like one product.
