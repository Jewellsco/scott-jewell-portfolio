A real bank · Before and after

One bank's website, before and after.

CoBank is a large American bank owned by its own customers, mostly farmers and rural businesses. The top screen recreates the online banking site those customers use today, built from the bank's own help guides. The bottom screen is my redesign, and everything in it works, click anything. The buttons under the screens walk through the biggest differences one at a time. I do not work for CoBank, this is an independent design exercise built from public information.

After, my redesign, everything works, click around

Why I designed it this way. CoBank's customers are not just customers, they each own a piece of the bank. So this screen leads with the person, what you own, what the bank paid you back, when you get a vote, and how it reaches you. Every number explains itself in plain words, one tap away, and nothing they already had was lost, the balances and reports are all still here, just easier to read.
